# General Grading Rubric for Engineering Modules

## Overview

Each module report is evaluated on **five core competencies** that reflect essential engineering communication skills. This rubric applies to all modules, though specific technical content and weighting varies by module.

**Total Points per Module: 100**

---

## Grading Categories

### 1. Technical Analysis and Calculations (~30 points)

This category assesses your ability to correctly apply chemical principles, perform calculations, and use appropriate methods.

#### Goal
- All calculations are correct with proper setup and methodology clearly shown
- Chemical principles (stoichiometry, thermodynamics, equilibrium, etc.) applied correctly
- Appropriate models and equations selected for the problem
- All assumptions stated and justified
- Spreadsheet formulas (when applicable) are correct and well-organized
- Minor errors only, if any

#### Avoid
- Fundamental calculation errors
- Major misunderstanding of chemical principles
- Inappropriate methods or no clear methodology
- Missing explicit requirements
- Analysis is not viable

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Show your work (formulas, setup, logic)
- Use correct units throughout
- Check reasonableness of results (does a 10,000Â°C temperature make sense?)
- State and justify any assumptions you make

---

### 2. Data Interpretation and Critical Thinking (~25 points)

This category evaluates your ability to analyze data, identify patterns, draw valid conclusions, and think critically about results.

#### Goal
- Data correctly interpreted with clear identification of key trends and patterns
- Results compared to appropriate standards, baselines, or theoretical predictions
- Quantitative statements throughout (specific values with units, not vague descriptions)
- Discrepancies or unexpected results identified and explained
- Alternative explanations considered where appropriate
- Demonstrates deep understanding of what the data reveals about the system


#### Avoid
- Major misinterpretation of data
- No meaningful comparison to standards
- Entirely qualitative with no quantitative support
- Results not analyzed for reasonableness
- No critical thinking evident
- Fundamental misunderstanding of data

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Always include specific values with units (not "contamination increased" but "Fe increased from 0.2 ppm to 12.5 ppm, a 62x increase")
- Compare your results to industry standards, theoretical predictions, or expected values
- Ask yourself: Do these results make physical/chemical sense?
- Identify trends across conditions (how does X change as Y increases?)
- Don't ignore outliers or unexpected results - explain them

---

### 3. Engineering Judgment and Recommendations (~20 points)

This category assesses your ability to make sound engineering decisions, consider practical constraints, and provide actionable recommendations.

#### Goal
- Recommendations are specific, actionable, and directly supported by data analysis
- Multiple valid approaches considered with clear tradeoff analysis
- Practical constraints (cost, time, feasibility, accuracy) explicitly addressed
- Priorities clearly identified and justified
- Decisions demonstrate sophisticated understanding of engineering context
- Verification/validation plan included for proposed solutions


#### Avoid
- No clear recommendations or recommendations contradict data
- No analysis of options
- No consideration of practical constraints
- No clear decision-making framework
- Recommendations not viable or inappropriate
- No thought given to implementation

**Key Focus Areas:**
- Be specific: "Inspect thermal furnace heating elements for corrosion" not "improve equipment"
- Acknowledge tradeoffs: "UV-Vis is faster and cheaper but ICP-MS provides definitive identification"
- Consider feasibility: Is your recommendation realistic given constraints?
- Justify your choices: Why is this the best approach?
- Think about verification: How would you confirm your solution worked?
- Recognize when multiple approaches are valid

---

### 4. Communication and Presentation (~15 points)

This category evaluates how effectively you communicate your analysis through writing and visualizations.

#### Goal
- Writing is clear, concise, and professional with minimal grammatical errors
- Document is well-organized with logical flow between sections
- Figures are clear, properly labeled (axes, units, legends), and professionally formatted
- Figure captions are descriptive and informative
- Tables are properly formatted with clear headers and consistent formatting
- All units clearly indicated and consistent throughout
- Technical terminology used correctly
- Document formatted according to assignment requirements 

#### Avoid
- Writing is very unclear or unprofessional
- No clear document organization
- Figures are unlabeled, unreadable, or missing
- No figure captions
- Any necessary Tables are absent or unusable
- Units missing throughout
- Technical terminology not understood
- Formatting requirements ignored
- Too verbose or pedagogical (this is not a lab report!)

**Key Focus Areas:**
- **Figures must have:**
  - Clear axis labels with units
  - Descriptive titles or captions
  - Legible text and symbols
  - Appropriate scale and formatting
  - Referenced in your text ("as shown in Figure 1...")

- **Units must be:**
  - Present on all numerical values
  - Consistent throughout the document
  - Appropriate for the quantity (ppm for concentrations, kJ/mol for energies, etc.)

- **Writing should be:**
  - Concise and direct (avoid unnecessary words)
  - Professional in tone (engineering memo, not casual email)
  - Free of major grammatical errors
  - Organized logically with clear section headers

---

### 5. Professionalism and Scholarship (~10 points)

This category assesses attention to detail, proper attribution, and overall professionalism.

#### Goal
- All sources properly cited in a consistent format
- Citations include textbook sections, online resources, and any AI tools used
- Document follows all submission requirements (file format, naming, zip structure)
- Work submitted on time
- No evidence of academic integrity violations
- Demonstrates careful attention to detail throughout

#### Avoid
- Failing to cite any sources you make use of
- Submission format completely wrong
- Very late or missing submission
- Academic integrity violations
- Work appears rushed or careless

**Key Focus Areas:**
- **Citations required for:**
  - Textbook chapters or sections used
  - Online resources consulted
  - Any AI assistance used (specify purpose: "AI used for proofreading and checking unit consistency")
  - Data sources or reference materials

- **Submission checklist:**
  - Correct file format (markdown with linked images in zip file)
  - Proper filename: ModuleX_LastName_FirstName.zip
  - All images included and properly linked
  - Submitted by deadline
  - All sections complete

- **Academic integrity:**
  - Show your own work and thinking
  - Cite all sources of assistance
  - If you consulted with classmates, acknowledge it
  - AI should enhance your learning, not replace your effort

## Example Score Interpretation

**90-100 points:** Excellent work demonstrating strong technical skills, clear communication, sound engineering judgment, and professional presentation. Minor improvements possible but work is of high quality.

**80-89 points:** Good work with solid technical analysis and communication. Some areas for improvement in depth of analysis, clarity of presentation, or completeness of recommendations.

**70-79 points:** Satisfactory work showing basic competence but with significant room for improvement in technical accuracy, depth of analysis, or communication quality.

**60-69 points:** Work meets minimal standards but has substantial deficiencies in multiple categories. Requires significant improvement.

**Below 60 points:** Work does not meet basic standards for technical accuracy, analysis quality, or professional presentation.

## Tips for Success

### Before You Start
1. Read the module instructions carefully - all of them
2. Understand what data you have and what you need to calculate
3. Set up any tools, software, etc. in advance
4. Plan your analysis approach before diving into calculations

### During Your Work
1. Check your calculations for reasonableness (do the units make sense? Are magnitudes reasonable?)
2. Create figures as you go - don't wait until the end
3. Take notes on observations and insights as they occur
4. Keep track of sources you consult

### Before You Submit
1. Proofread your entire document
2. Verify all figures display correctly and are properly labeled
3. Check that all numerical values have units
4. Ensure citations are complete
5. Review the rubric to make sure you've addressed all criteria
6. Test that your zip file contains all necessary files
